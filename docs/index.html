The Problem

Resistance is outrunning the lab.

Antibiotic resistance is one of the fastest-moving crises in modern medicine. Ciprofloxacin — once a first-line treatment for gonorrhea — went from 0% resistance in the 1980s to 46% today.

Mapping resistance across thousands of bacterial strains is slow and expensive — every lab test costs time, materials, and trained personnel. The question isn't just "which strains are resistant?" It's "which strain should I test next to learn the most, as fast as possible?"

The Method

Active learning, with uncertainty sampling.

AcquireML treats antibiotic resistance as a black-box optimization problem. Not every experiment teaches the model equally — choosing strategically means needing far fewer of them.

1

Seed with a small batch

The lab runs an initial set of experiments — e.g. 10 bacterial strains.

2

Train on those results

AcquireML fits a model to whatever labeled data exists so far.

3

Chart the unexplored pool

It scans every untested strain and finds the ones the model is most uncertain about.

4

Recommend the next batch

Those uncertain strains become the next round of recommended experiments.

5

Run them, feed results back

The lab tests the batch, reveals the results, and the model retrains immediately.

6

Repeat until it plateaus

Accuracy compounds each round, until a stopping rule signals the data has taught what it can.

What the model learns

AcquireML doesn't just produce a number — it explains which DNA fragments drive resistance.
